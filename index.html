Computational Creativity · University of Leicester

Can machines create — and can they judge what's creative?

We study Large Language Models and multi-agent AI systems that generate creative artefacts — and evaluate them. Led by Dr Fabrício Góes and Dr Marco Volpe.

The work

Three threads run through everything we do

From jokes and poetry to patents and games, our projects orbit a single question: what does it take for a machine to be creative, and for us to trust its judgement?

01

Evaluating creativity

Can models judge novelty, value, surprise and suspense the way people do? We build panels of AI judges and test them against experts — on jokes, poetry, and alternative uses.

02

Generating with agents

Multi-agent LLM systems that ideate rather than autocomplete — inventing patents, game narratives, believable characters and card decks through structured collaboration.

03

Keeping humans in the loop

Aligning machine output with expert voices and keeping people in control — safety for young audiences, agreement studies, and mentorship that builds human judgement.

PhD researchers

Five projects, five frontiers

The doctoral researchers turning these questions into systems, studies and prototypes.

Science communication

Jacob Watson

Supporting science educators at the National Space Centre with AI that gives safe, accurate and engaging answers to questions from 8–9 year-old visitors — always with a human in the loop.

National Space Centre · Q&A
AI & storytelling

Rehan Malik

Helping screenwriters interact with believable AI characters while they write. A memory system grounded in each character's mood keeps personalities progressing — instead of drifting towards inconsistency.

Film · character memory
Automated invention

Theo Wong

Automating patent creation: AI reads existing patents, organises them into trees of features and functionalities, maps current solutions, and uses multi-agent ideation to propose genuinely novel inventions.

Patents · ideation
Evaluation & agreement

Abdullah Al Rabeyah

Do open models, closed models and humans agree on novelty, value and feasibility? Studying where judgements converge — and how human–AI multi-agent panels reach better creativity decisions.

Agreement · multi-agent judging
Game design

Baiyang Qu

Narrative effects in continuous games via multi-agent systems — formally guaranteeing surprise and suspense from the game state: what characters know, the items they hold, the environment and history.

Games · surprise & suspense

Beyond the lab

Two initiatives we're building

Where the research meets practice — in education and in cultural heritage.

MentorplAI

If AI absorbs the execution that used to teach people judgement, how does the next generation still learn to be good? Mentorplai reshapes the ladder — mentoring people through the tools, not just with the answers they give.

OLA Digital

The Latin American Research Observatory at the Institute for Digital Culture. Mapping the region's digital cultural repositories — institutions, datasets and needs — and building federated systems to share knowledge.
